Analysis of Retail Store Sales Data: A Power BI Dashboard Project

Exploration of Sales Trends, Customer Behavior, and Product Performance Metrics using Power BI

Introduction

In this project, I took on the challenge of analyzing sales data from a retail store called SuperStore to uncover trends and performance metrics related to orders, product categories and segments, and customer market. By examining various features such as sales amount, units sold, and shipping costs and methods. I aim to create a dashboard meant to display general information for the business shareholders.

Data Overview

The dataset from Kaggle consists of order records from the retail store and includes the following features:

  • Order Date: The date when the order was placed.
  • Ship Date: The date the order was shipped.
  • Ship Mode: The shipping method used for each order (Standard Class, First Class, Second Class, Same Day).
  • Customer Name: The name of the customer who placed the order.
  • Segment: The segment to which the customer belongs (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office).
  • State: The U.S. state where the customer is located.
  • Market: The broader market region (Africa, APAC, Canada, EMEA, EU, LATAM, US).
  • Region: Specific regions within the market (North, Central, Southeast Asia).
  • Product ID: Unique identifier for each product sold.
  • Category: The category of the product (Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology).
  • Sub-Category: A more granular classification of product categories.
  • Product Name: The name of the product sold.
  • Sales Amount: The total revenue generated from each sale.
  • Quantity Sold: The number of units sold per order.
  • Discount: The discount applied to each sale.
  • Profit: The profit generated from each sale.
  • Shipping Cost: The cost associated with shipping the order.
  • Order Priority: The priority assigned to the order (Low, Medium, High, Critical).
  • Year: The year in which the order was placed.

Methodology

  1. Data Cleaning: Filtering out incomplete or inconsistent records to ensure the dataset's integrity.
  2. Data Aggregation: Summarizing sales data by various dimensions, such as product category, customer segment, and time periods.
  3. Performance Metrics Calculation: Calculating key performance indicators (KPIs) including total sales, total profit, average profit margin, and shipping costs.
  4. Visualization: Creating dashboards and visual representations of the data to highlight trends and performance metrics.

Dashboard

KPI section shows values we want to know depending on our selection on the dashboard. The filter pane adjusts the independent variables - city, house type, and storey level. The parameter section lets us choose a metric either Median Price in millions of Pesos, Price per square meter in thousands of Pesos, or Total Listings.

Insights

Median Prices

Based on property listings from Lamudi, the median house price in Metro Manila was Php 28.65 million, with a median price of Php 212,580 per square meter. Makati City, known for its central business district, stood out with a staggering median price of Php 152 million and an average of Php 643,600 per square meter. Taguig, home to the upscale Bonifacio Global City, followed closely with a median price of Php 112 million and Php 280,500 per square meter. Other cities, such as Marikina (Php 9.7M), Valenzuela (Php 7.4M), and Caloocan (Php 4.5M), offered the most inexpensive options.
