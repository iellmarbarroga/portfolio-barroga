Visualizing Dengue Trends in the Philippines: A Power BI Dashboard Analysis

Exploring Key Metrics, Seasonal Patterns, and Regional Insights from Five Years of Dengue Data in the country

Introduction

One of the most pressing public health concerns in tropical countries like the Philippines is dengue fever, a mosquito-borne disease that affects thousands of individuals annually. For this project, I took on the challenge of exploring the dataset on Dengue Cases in the Philippines and created a Power BI dashboard to visualize trends, patterns, and regional impacts of the disease. As part of my data analyst portfolio, I analyzed five years' worth of dengue case data in the Philippines using Power BI to uncover trends and insights. This dashboard visualizes key metrics, regional patterns, and seasonal impacts.

Data Overview

The dataset, sourced from the Philippine Department of Health (DOH) via Kaggle, covers dengue cases and deaths from 2016-2020. The main columns include:

  • Month: Text
  • Year: (2016-2020)
  • Region: (Philippine regions)
  • Dengue_Cases: (monthly case count)
  • Dengue_Deaths: (monthly death count)

In preprocessing, I standardized region names, mapped regions to island groups (Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao) and its corresponding populations, and categorized months into rainy (June-November) and dry seasons (December-May).

KPIs

To provide a concise overview, I included four critical KPIs in the dashboard:

  1. Total Cases: Sum of all reported dengue cases (2016-2020).
  2. Total Deaths: Sum of all deaths attributed to dengue.
  3. Fatality Rate: Calculated as Total Deaths / Total Cases, indicating the lethality of the disease.
  4. Prevalence Rate: Calculated as Total Cases / Population for each region, showing how widespread dengue is relative to population size.

Dashboard

KPI section shows values and its year-quarter trend that we want to know depending on our selection on the dashboard. The filter pane adjusts the independent variables - year, group island, and region. The parameter section lets us choose a metric either Total Cases or Total Deaths distribution.

Insights

Regional Trends

Luzon had the highest number of dengue cases (597k) due to its overall population, greater than the cases from Visayas and Mindanao combined (280k and 269k respectively). Regions in Luzon, particularly Region IV-A (CALABARZON), Region III (Central Luzon) and Metro Manila had the highest number of cases.

For total deaths, Luzon (6.8k) still had the highest number of deaths, followed relatively closely by Mindanao (5.9k). Visayas had the lowest recorded deaths (4.3k).

The average fatality and prevalence rate are 1.47% and 1.11% respectively. Luzon had 1.14% and 0.96% fatality and prevalence rate, the lowest among the three group islands. Mindanao had the highest fatality rate of 2.18% followed by Visayas with 1.48%. On the other hand, Visayas has the highest prevalence rate of 1.77% followed by Mindanao with 1.07%.

However, Metro Manila had the highest fatality rate (3.46%) way greater than double the national average (1.47%).

Seasonal Impact

As expected, dengue cases surged during the rainy season (June-November), peaking around August and September. Dry season months saw fewer cases, but spikes were observed occasionally.

Population and Prevalence

Surprisingly, Metro Manila with highest population density had recorded lower prevalence rate (0.11%) as compared to the national average (1.11%). The prevalence rate is consistent with the regions with the highest populations (Region IV-A: 0.16%, Region III: 0.13%, Region VII: 0.11%)

Conclusion

The Power BI dashboard provides a detailed, interactive view of dengue trends in the Philippines. It offers actionable insights that can assist public health officials in targeted interventions. The inclusion of other metrics or KPIs like the fatality rate and prevalence rate further enriches the analysis, making it a valuable tool for understanding the scope of dengue outbreaks.
